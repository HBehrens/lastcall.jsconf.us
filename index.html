Dear JS Community,

I am Chris Williams, co-creator of JSConf, and I have some rather sad news to share with you. My co-creator for this event and more importantly my wife Laura Williams has suffered from a very severe neurological infection of which she is currently recovering through with no short term memory. I have to focus on caring for her and helping her and our family to recover through this very scary and very stressful time. Unfortunately, in 2014 we contracted our venue for 2 years not having a clue anything like this could ever happen. I had built JSConf US in a manner that it could help other conferences out of such a catastrophe, never thinking I would be the one to need it most, but alas here I am.

I have already removed myself from any and all of my technical obligations and ownerships, tranferring them to individuals I trust and greatly appreciate. At this point, within the tech field, there are only two things directly connected to me, RobotsConf and JSConf. I, and the amazing people who help me (Ken Henderson, Derek Lindahl, and Tara Lindahl among many others), cannot confidently pull off another RobotsConf this year with everything on my plate. I have made the decision to put on a final call event of JSConf US over December 4th and 5th and provide this community with one last gathering curated by me for the foreseeable future. This event will simultaneously be very much a JSConf US, while also having to shed many of the wonderful things that Laura brought to the event to make it special. This event will be smaller because it needs to be in order for me to accomplish it without letting the quality of life and care for my wife and family to suffer. It will not be able to provide for everyone's unique needs because for once, I have to first and foremost provide for my own unique needs. If that offends or upsets, I openly apologize, but I cannot provide anything more than that.

I have given this community pretty much every bit of my energy, being, and ability to this community. I have nothing but love and respect for every single person involved in web community and if I could continue on I would. Unfortunately it was not in the cards for that to happen, so this event will have to be my goodbye until things drastically change for the better.

With many tears,

Chris Williams
